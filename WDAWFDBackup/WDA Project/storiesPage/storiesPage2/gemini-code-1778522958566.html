As we cross the threshold of 8 billion people, the story of global demographics is no longer just about growth—it is about a radical divergence. While some nations are bracing for a population explosion, others are managing an unprecedented contraction.

The Great Population Divergence

By 2050, over half of the world’s projected population growth will be concentrated in just eight countries. This shift represents a fundamental change in the global economic and political landscape.

While sub-Saharan Africa continues to see robust growth, East Asia and Europe are entering a "super-aged" phase. Japan, Italy, and South Korea are at the forefront of this transition, facing challenges that will soon become global norms.

Urbanization: The Century of the City

In 1950, only 30% of the world lived in urban areas. Today, that number is 56%, and it is expected to reach nearly 70% by 2050. The story of demographics is increasingly a story of the city.
